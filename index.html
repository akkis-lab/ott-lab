NRF2 signaling pathway

Under basal condition, KEAP1 controlled NRF2 undergoes Cul3 mediated ubiquitination, leading to the proteosomal degradation of NRF2. Under oxidative stress, the NRF2 dissociates from KEAP1 and enters the nucleus where it heterodimerises with the sMaf proteins and regulates the ARE driven gene expression.

Fate of NRF2 in normal cells and cancer cells.

Loss of KEAP1 function leads to defective activation of the NRF2 signaling pathway in NSCLC cells, resulting in resistance to chemotherapeutic agents.

Omics-To-Therapy (OTT) Group

The Omics-To-Therapy (OTT) Group is dedicated to translating multi-omics discoveries into precision cancer therapies. We integrate genomics, transcriptomics, epigenomics, and proteomics with computational biology and drug discovery to bridge the gap between molecular insights and clinical applications.

  • Part of the Department of Molecular Oncology at Sri Shankara Cancer Hospital & Research Centre (SSCHRC).
  • Collaborates with oncologists and clinicians for research with patient-derived samples and real-world data.
  • Supported by state-of-the-art laboratory facilities for cutting-edge studies in cancer diagnostics and treatment.

We are an integral part of Sri Shankara National Centre for Cancer Prevention and Research (SSNCCPR), under the Sri Shankara Cancer Foundation (SSCF), Bangalore.
