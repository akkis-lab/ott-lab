Research at Omics To Therapeutics Lab

Decoding Cancer Through Omics & Precision Therapeutics

At OTT Lab, we explore the intricate molecular mechanisms of cancer progression, drug resistance, and precision oncology. Our research integrates genomics, transcriptomics, epigenomics, and proteomics to uncover novel biomarkers, therapeutic targets, and treatment strategies.

Key Research Areas

Investigating how NRF2-Keap1 dysregulation drives tumorigenesis and therapy resistance.

Developing strategies to target NRF2 in drug-resistant cancers.

Exploring oxidative stress regulation and its impact on cancer metabolism.

Identifying key mutations, copy number variations (CNVs), gene fusions, and structural variants in tumors.

Integrating Indian population-specific genomic data to study ethnicity-based cancer mutations.

Using AI-driven computational models for biomarker discovery.

Identifying clinically actionable mutations for personalized therapy.

Predicting drug responses and resistance mechanisms using computational biology.

Developing AI-driven patient stratification models for targeted treatments.

Investigating immune evasion mechanisms and their role in immunotherapy resistance.

Screening small molecules and repurposed drugs for novel cancer therapies.

Exploring combination therapy strategies to overcome drug resistance.

Why does it Matter?

✔ Advancing Personalized Cancer Treatment – Bridging the gap between cancer genomics and clinical therapeutics.

✔ Bringing Indian Genomics to the Forefront – Using Indian population data to uncover unique cancer biomarkers.

✔ Targeting Drug Resistance – Developing new approaches to combat therapy resistance and improve patient survival.
